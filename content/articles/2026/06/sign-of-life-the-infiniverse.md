# Sign of Life: The Infiniverse

In my previous post, [Sign of Life: Domain of Discourse](./sign-of-life-domain-of-discourse.md),
I went to sleep hoping the boundaries of my sandbox universe would neatly map
themselves out. Well, it did not.

I was rigging the game by designing *Krit* for the environment or the
environment for *Krit*.


## Sandbox Universe
To break away from my own cognitive biases, I went back to a foundational
framework from Jain philosophy: splitting reality into *Tattva* (Entities) and
*Guna* (Properties). In simple terms: what exists and what can it do?

- **Space**: The container. Jainism says there is only one, flat 3D space.
But why stop there? My sandbox needs to allow for an infinite number of spaces,
shifting dimensions (from 1D lines to hyper-shapes) or space points that pack
together with varying density.

- **Time**: The clock cycle. Does it only tick forward? Can it run backward in
certain pockets? Does every space point carry its own local clock, causing time
to warp depending on where you stand?

- **Matter**: The stuff inside. Can packets of matter merge, split or pass
right through each other, does behavior of matter change due to space or time?

- **Nature**: The laws. The mathematical bridge that dictates how matter moves
(or does not move) through space over time.

By turning every physical law into an adjustable parameter, the combinations
explode into uncountable variations. This is the Infiniverse.

One universe is just a tiny, warped subset of another.


## Invariant Intelligence
This architecture forces a radical shift in how I define intelligence. If the
physical rules of the Infiniverse can change infinitely, then true intelligence
must hold under all subset universes.

If I say *Krit* is intelligent because it masters its environment in a 3D space,
but it breaks down the moment I flatten the world into 2D, then *Krit* was never
intelligent.

This is where my earlier hypothesis, the Spectrum of Intelligence, plays an
important role. If I push *Krit* from a 3D space into a 4D pocket, a new,
completely unknown parameter will enter the equation. Matter might appear to
vanish and reappear out of thin air.
When that happens, I do not need *Krit* to instantly win, survive or dominate
this higher dimension. I just need it to comprehend.

Intelligence is bound by the limits of the physical world it currently occupies,
but the capacity for intelligence must be invariant.
